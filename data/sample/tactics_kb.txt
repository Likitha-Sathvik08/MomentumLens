SOCCER TACTICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE

== Substitution Effects ==
Substitutions typically occur at 60-75 minutes and can shift momentum significantly.
Fresh legs increase pressing intensity and can destabilize an opponent's defensive shape.
A defensive substitution signals a team switching to a low-block or 5-at-the-back shape.
A forward substitution signals increased attacking intent, often shifting to 4-3-3 or 3-4-3.

== Red Card Impact ==
A red card forces the affected team into a defensive block, conceding possession.
The team with numerical advantage typically switches to a wider formation to exploit space.
Momentum almost always shifts to the team gaining the numerical advantage after a red card.

== High Pressing Phases ==
High pressing (gegenpressing) forces turnovers in dangerous areas, creating quick transitions.
When a team wins 3+ tackles in 5 minutes, it usually signals a pressing trap being set.
Pressing success rate above 30% in a window correlates with momentum shifts.

== Possession Shifts ==
Sustained possession above 65% over 10 minutes indicates dominance.
Loss of possession in the final third often triggers a counter-attacking shift.
Recycling possession through a defensive midfielder ("double pivot") signals defensive consolidation.

== Set Piece Momentum ==
Goals from set pieces often shift momentum as they change game state and crowd energy.
A team conceding a set piece goal in their own box frequently drops into a deeper defensive line.

== Tactical Patterns ==
4-2-3-1: High pressing with midfield control. Vulnerable to wide overloads.
4-3-3: Wing-dominant. Gains momentum through 1v1 dribbles and overloads.
3-5-2: Compact mid-block with wing-back attacks. Shifts momentum through transition.
5-4-1: Defensive shape designed to absorb pressure; counter-attack focused.
